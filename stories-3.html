About the project

We (Loana and Tim) need your help! We are currently working on a data collection on sexist advertising. Unfortunately, it wasn't really difficult to create the collection, because capitalism provides us with masses of problematic advertising. But how do you deal with such a topic without reproducing the sexist (image) language? What we want: A collection of descriptions of the different posters to show that it doesn't even need the image to show the problematic nature of the content. What we need from you: A short description (30 sec to 5 mins) of the story of a random poster we send you. Simply send a voice message to Loana or Tim. Important: We are not interested in the formal structure of the picture (we don't care about font, composition etc.), but in how you would describe the picture to a person who doesn't see it: What do you see, what are your associations, what is the message, what is problematic. The aim is to get as many people as possible to participate, so that we have a wide range of different descriptions.

What now?

What can we do against the omnipresent capitalisation of sexism and patriarcal discrimination? Naturally, there is not one big answer to that question, no masterplan to fight the oppressing system. But all together, with a web of small, connected solutions, we might come closer to what we think the world should be like. Intersectionalism is of course an important pillar of this network, because every fight against discrimination is a valid one, since different forms of discrimination often overlay in our society and thereby create the pyramid scheme of priviledges from which we want to escape.

Below you will find some links that we considered useful in terms of information and involvement of everyone visiting this site. These are either NGOs who are engaging in women's and lgbtqia+ rights or detailed information on sexism in advertising. We encourage you to send us a message if you want to add some info or organisation to the list. Thank you!
